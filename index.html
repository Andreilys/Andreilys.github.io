About Me

Throughout my short life, I've had the opportunity to explore a variety of jobs and cities. From Beijing to Los Angeles, a Fortune 500 company to a Y Combinator funded startup, I've been exposed to a remarkable amount of interesting people and opportunities. It wasn't until recently that I realized how Data Science offers a perfect blend of all my interests - programming, data, and story telling. As a result I've been spending more and more time learning about Machine Learning, Data Engineering, Data Visualization and Data Analytics in hopes of working in a full time Data Science role upon graduating in April 2019. My long term aim is to apply data science and machine learning to personal information (health, financial, social, etc.) in order to better improve personal decision making that will lead people to living happier and more fulfilling lives.

In the past, I've worked as a McDonalds Swing Manager, ran an $86,000 house painting business, worked on growth and strategy in Beijing for IBM, managed the campus ambassador and influencer program for Soylent, and extracted insights as an analyst at Bank of Montreal.

In my spare time you can find me writing on Quora, playing chess, reading, meditating, or deepening my understanding of Buddhism and Stoicism.

What I'm Doing Now

Last updated: January, 2018
The easiest way to see what I'm currently doing is to checkout my personal dashboard.

I'm starting my second semester in Computer Science as a junior at Queen's University. The majority of my early semester will be dedicated to finding an internship in data science, machine learning or data engineering ideally in either in San Francisco or Los Angeles.

The projects I'm currently working on are crypto signals, an open source automated technical analysis tool for cryptocurrencies, and Sicana, which is an SMS based service that allows users who lack internet to access facts and crowdsourced answers to common questions as part of the Hult Prize competition.
